# NorthStar Hero Redesign Plan (Conversion-First)

## Objective
Redesign only the hero section so the value proposition is immediately clear and conversion-oriented.

## Why the previous hero failed
- It used abstract system language that did not communicate buyer value fast enough.
- It emphasized motion before business outcomes.
- It did not anchor the first screen around speed, quality, and risk reduction benefits.

## Web research signals (competitors)

### What top staffing/recruiting competitors emphasize
- Faster hiring outcomes and access to qualified talent.
- Clear trust framing and confidence in hiring decisions.
- Transformation/partnership language tied to measurable business results.

## Inference from research
- Competitor heroes convert better when they lead with tangible outcomes (faster hiring, higher confidence, reduced risk).
- Dribbble concepts perform better visually when motion is tied to a clear story (pipeline/progression), not random ambient widgets.

## Hero concept selected for V1
`Proof-to-Hire Engine`

### Story in one glance
Source -> Verify -> Shortlist -> Interview Ready

### Above-the-fold structure
1. Outcome headline (speed + confidence)
2. 3 concrete benefits
3. Two CTAs (primary + secondary)
4. Interactive right panel with role scenarios
5. Live metrics and animated candidate flow tied to verification outcomes

## UX interaction rules
- Motion must reinforce business meaning (candidate progression and risk filtering).
- Interactivity should change outcomes in real-time (scenario tabs update metrics and statuses).
- No long accordion content in hero.
- All text must maintain high contrast and immediate readability.
